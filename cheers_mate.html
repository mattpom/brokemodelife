Bar Tab Strategy

Drink Well for Less

✦ Always True

Five Moves That Cut Your Drinking Budget in Half

  • Buy handles not bottles — The 1.75L handle is 30 to 40 percent cheaper per ounce than the 750ml bottle. Do the math once and never buy small again.
  • Learn three cocktails really well — Old Fashioned, Margarita, Gin and Tonic. Know these cold and you never overpay at a bar for basics again.
  • Trader Joe's wine section — Their $5 to $8 wines consistently outperform bottles costing three times as much in blind tastings. Shop there first.
  • Happy hour is a lifestyle — Most bars offer 50 percent off from 4 to 6 PM. Shift your schedule 90 minutes and cut your bar tab in half permanently.
  • Pre-game smart, not sloppy — One or two drinks at home before going out saves $20 to $40 per night while still fully enjoying the social experience.

💡 Drinking well on a budget is about knowing what you actually like and buying more of exactly that.

Home Bar

Build It for Under $50

✦ Always True

The Only Equipment You Actually Need

  • One quality spirit, bought large — Pick your base spirit and buy the 1.75L. That is your foundation for every cocktail you will ever make at home.
  • Fresh citrus changes everything — A squeezed lime or lemon transforms a mediocre cocktail into a genuinely good one. A bag of limes costs $2.
  • Simple syrup is 5 cents to make — Equal parts sugar and hot water, stir until dissolved. Costs almost nothing and lasts two weeks in the fridge.
  • Bitters are a one-time purchase — Angostura bitters costs $8 and lasts years. Adds depth to whiskey drinks that would otherwise taste completely flat.
  • A good shaker is $15 and lasts forever — That is the only equipment you actually need. Shake it, strain it, serve it. Everything else is optional.

💡 A home bar that cost $50 to build will save you $500 in bar tabs over the course of a year.
