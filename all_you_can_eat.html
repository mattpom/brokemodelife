Broke Mode Strategy

The $1 Meal Formula

✦ Always True

Master These Five and Your Food Budget Drops by Half

  • Rice and beans = complete protein — Under $1 per meal. Covers all essential amino acids. Infinite variations with different seasonings and toppings.
  • Frozen veg beats fresh on a budget — Picked at peak ripeness, nutritionally equal, and 60 percent cheaper. Fresh for salads, frozen for everything cooked.
  • Eggs at every meal — About 15 cents each. The cheapest complete protein on earth. Works for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks.
  • Batch cook once, eat five days — Two hours on Sunday makes rice, roasted veg, and a protein. Mix and match all week with near-zero daily effort.
  • Shop the perimeter last — Hit bulk and dry goods first. Fill your cart with cheap staples before reaching the expensive packaged center aisles.

💡 The cheapest meal you will ever eat is the one you made from what was already in your kitchen.

No Waste System

Treat Leftovers Like Assets

✦ Always True

Nothing Goes in the Trash If It Can Go in a New Dish

  • Label everything — Masking tape and a sharpie. Write what it is and the date. Your future self will thank you every single time.
  • Pre-split into grab-and-go containers — Divide meals right after cooking so they are ready to grab throughout the week without thinking.
  • Leftover rice becomes stir fry — Day-old rice is better for stir fry than fresh. Add egg, frozen veg, and soy sauce. Dinner in 8 minutes.
  • Soft produce goes in the freezer — Bananas going brown, wilting spinach, soft berries all go in the freezer before they go in the trash.
  • One tray bake per week — One starch, one protein, three vegetables roasted together. Three meals worth of components done in one session.

💡 Broke mode is about attention, not deprivation. Pay attention to what you have before buying more.
