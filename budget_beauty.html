Budget Beauty Tips

The Foundation

Three Products, Full Routine

✦ Always True

Everything Else Is Optional

  • Cleanser — CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser — Dermatologist-recommended, gentle for all skin types, under $15. This is genuinely all you need to clean your face properly.
  • Moisturizer — Neutrogena Hydro Boost — Lightweight, non-greasy, works under makeup or alone. Under $18 and outperforms products costing five times as much.
  • SPF — EltaMD UV Clear — The product dermatologists universally agree prevents premature aging. Worth the $35 splurge. Apply every single day without exception.
  • Nothing else is required — The beauty industry sells you the idea that a 12-step routine is necessary. Three steps done consistently beats twelve done randomly.
  • Introduce one new product at a time — Multiple new products at once makes it impossible to identify what caused a breakout or reaction. One at a time, always.

💡 Skincare is a consistency game, not a product game. Three cheap products used daily beat twenty expensive ones used occasionally.

Shop Strategy

Where to Find the Good Stuff

✦ Always True

Five Places That Beat the Drugstore Price

  • Marshalls beauty aisle first — Name-brand skincare at 50 to 70 percent off. Stock changes every week. Check it every single time you are nearby.
  • CVS clearance bins are underrated — End-of-season clearance goes as low as 75 percent off. Products are completely fine, just discontinued packaging or shades.
  • Ulta 21 Days of Beauty twice yearly — Prestige products go 50 percent off in a rotating daily sale. Plan your wishlist in advance and buy on the correct day.
  • Amazon Subscribe and Save — Set up recurring orders for razors and moisturizer. The 15 percent discount is applied automatically without any effort.
  • Sephora samples on request — Walk in and ask for samples before committing to buying anything. Most associates give 3 to 5 samples without hesitation.

💡 The beauty industry is built on convincing you that price equals quality. Blind studies consistently prove it rarely does.
