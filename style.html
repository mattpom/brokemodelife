Five Rules That Make You Look Like You Spent More

  • Fit is everything — A $10 thrift store blazer altered to fit beats a $200 off-the-rack blazer that doesn't. Find a local tailor. Cost is $10 to $20.
  • Neutral color palette only — Black, white, grey, navy, tan. Everything matches everything. You look intentional with half the wardrobe size.
  • Thrift basics, buy quality once — T-shirts, jeans, and chinos from thrift stores. Spend what you saved on one quality piece that elevates every outfit around it.
  • Depop and Poshmark for brands — Designer items at 70 to 90 percent off. Know your measurements and online thrifting becomes a genuine superpower.
  • Shoe care extends life by years — A $5 brush and polish turns $40 shoes into shoes that look like $120. Clean shoes signal everything about you.

💡 Style is mostly confidence. Confidence is mostly fit. Fit costs $15 at a tailor.
