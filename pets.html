The Broke Mode Pet Parent Guide

✦ Always True

Five Ways to Spoil Your Pet Without Breaking the Bank

  • Chewy Autoship saves 35 percent — Set recurring orders for food, treats, and flea prevention. The discount is automatic and shipping is free every time.
  • Low-cost vet clinics exist everywhere — PetSmart and Petco partner with low-cost clinics for vaccines at 50 to 70 percent below private vet prices.
  • Preventative care is always cheaper — Monthly flea prevention, dental chews, and regular nail trims prevent the emergency vet bills that actually hurt your wallet.
  • DIY toys work just as well — A cardboard box, paper bag, or crinkled foil ball. Cats especially do not care about price tags. They care about play.
  • Pet insurance for young animals — $30 to $50 per month for a puppy or kitten pays for itself on the first emergency. Do not wait until rates go up.

💡 Your pet does not know what their toys cost. They know if you showed up. Show up.

Vet Bill Prevention

Keep Costs Down Before They Happen

✦ Always True

The Cheapest Vet Visit Is the One You Prevented

  • Annual wellness visit is non-negotiable — Catching problems early is always cheaper than treating them late. One annual visit prevents ten reactive visits later.
  • Brush their teeth three times per week — Dental disease is the most common and expensive pet health problem. A $5 toothbrush and pet toothpaste prevents it entirely.
  • Keep vaccinations current — Core vaccines protect against diseases that are expensive or fatal. Stay current and you eliminate those costs completely.
  • Weight management prevents everything — Obesity causes joint problems, diabetes, and heart disease in pets. Measure food portions every single meal without exception.
  • Know your emergency vet before you need it — Find the 24-hour emergency clinic near you now. Panic decisions in a crisis always cost significantly more than prepared ones.

💡 The cheapest vet visit is the one you never had to make because you prevented the problem first.
